# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished erhu-inspired two-string vertical fiddle standing upright as a real physical object, photographed in a clean neutral studio. The silhouette is the recognizable huqin profile: a slim vertical wooden neck rising from a small resonator body, two long strings running the full length of the neck, two tuning pegs set into the upper neck, and a qianjin nut-loop binding both strings to the neck partway up. The signature feature is the compact resonator at the base — a small drum-like sound box with a taut thin membrane stretched over its front face, a low bridge standing on the membrane where the two strings cross it, and the strings anchored below at a tailpiece spike. The bow rides between the two strings rather than over the top. Materials read as warm dark hardwood for the neck and body, a pale tensioned skin membrane on the playing face, simple wound metal strings, and a slender horsehair bow resting alongside. Realistic tabletop scale cues — the body sits roughly the size of a teacup, the neck about forearm length. Soft directional studio light, gentle falloff, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same erhu-inspired prototype staged on a maker's workbench in a quiet instrument workshop, leaning upright with the bow laid across the bench beside it and a few hand tools and a coil of string blurred in the background. Warm natural window light rakes across the membrane face and the grain of the wooden neck, shallow depth of field isolating the resonator and bridge, environmental storytelling of a setup-and-measure session in progress. Concept visualization only, photorealistic, no measurement callouts.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the resonator face: the small bridge standing on the taut thin membrane with the two strings passing over its top notches, the skin's fine surface texture and the subtle deflection where the bridge feet press into it, the warm hardwood rim of the sound box framing the membrane edge. Raking side light to bring out the membrane weave, the bridge profile, and the string-to-membrane coupling. High detail, photorealistic, shallow depth of field, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines or measurement callouts; no violin-family long-scale layout or chinrest; no impossible bridge or membrane mechanism; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
